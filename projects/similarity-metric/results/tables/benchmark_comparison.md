## Summary

This simulation compares nABCD against two common metrics:
- **SMD** (Standardized Mean Difference): Cohen's d
- **KS** (Kolmogorov-Smirnov statistic): Maximum CDF difference

**Sample size**: n = 100 per group
**Replications**: 200

Values shown as: Mean (SD)

## Key Findings

### 1. Location Shifts

All three metrics detect location shifts effectively. This is expected.

### 2. Scale Differences (Critical!)

When comparing N(50, 10) vs N(50, 15):
- **nABCD = 0.135**: Clear signal in "Small-Medium" range
- **SMD ≈ 0.11**: Near zero (noise) — **SMD is blind to scale**
- **KS = 0.169**: Detects but lacks interpretable scale

**nABCD advantage**: Detects variance differences that SMD misses.

### 3. Shape Differences (Critical!)

When comparing Normal vs Gamma with same mean and SD:
- **nABCD = 0.071**: Detects shape difference
- **SMD ≈ 0.12**: Near zero (noise) — **SMD is blind to shape**
- **KS = 0.121**: Detects but lacks interpretable scale

**nABCD advantage**: Captures full distributional information.

### 4. Combined Differences

When both location and scale differ, nABCD provides a single interpretable metric that captures the total distributional difference.
